Monogamy vs
Polygamy

Yes, this is still my resume!

But I promised it would be different and I will try not to fail your expectation. So I will start with a story and then you will understand what the intention is.

A while ago, while sitting in the coffee shop, at the next table were two men, about 30 years old, the coffee shop was not very busy and I started to pay attention to their conversation.

One said he only wanted one woman, while the other emphasized that he would like to marry more than one. The first one incredulously asks him: But why? Dealing with one woman is already so complicated, how can you want to have to deal with several?

I was curious as the conversation unfolded, as you can imagine... and especially what the argument would be to defend this idea.

He answered: "Sure, if you have more women, they will all have different abilities to solve different problems, which means you will have to worry a lot less."

I was amazed, not at the polygamist and monogamist perspective, but at how this idea fits our abilities.

Have you ever thought about how people with monogamous abilities always have an order to protect them, like lawyers, doctors, etc., while people with multiple abilities have no such protection? Examples? Entrepreneurs, marketers, programmers...

These people always need to be up to date with the latest news in the market! They are always learning, innovating and testing!

I LOVE IT!

I want to learn, I don't want to stand still, I don't want to have the feeling that if I lose my job I won't know how to do anything else.
